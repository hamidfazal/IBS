The Journey

From a clinical attachment to becoming a night RMO — a transformative path through psychiatry and rehabilitation.

2014 — The Beginning

The Mentor's Guidance

During my clinical attachment at Civil Hospital Karachi, Prof. Dr. Raza ur Rehman — Head of Department of Psychiatry and one of Pakistan's top psychiatrists — became my mentor. His kindness and generosity allowed me to learn without bounds. When I sought a part-time opportunity to support my educational expenses while preparing for the MRCPsych Part 1 exam, he guided me toward a reliever RMO position at IBS.

September 2014

The Interview & Training

I met Madam Shahla Ahmed, the administrator, whose support was instrumental in my joining. Following her, I was interviewed by Professor Sohail. I was asked to complete a two-week training period. Despite the distance and relying solely on public transport without a car, I committed to making it work.

October 1, 2014

Independent RMO

I began working independently as a Reliever RMO, covering duties whenever and wherever needed. This marked the true beginning of my hands-on experience in psychiatric care and drug rehabilitation.

Late 2014 — Early 2015

Fixed Night RMO

Management offered me a fixed night RMO position — 6 nights a week, with Sunday and gazetted holidays as 24-hour shifts with overtime. I spent more time at the hospital than at home, returning only for a few hours of sleep and chores before heading back each evening.

2014 — 2019

Five Years of Immersion

IBS was a psychiatry and drug rehabilitation center. Spending entire nights with patients — from their worst moments at admission through every stage of recovery — honed my diagnostic skills and deepened my understanding of mental illness and addiction. I learned to recognize stages, factors, and recovery patterns quickly, and to provide emergency measures with confidence.

During Tenure

Letter of Appreciation

A distinguished achievement: I received a Letter of Appreciation from Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan, co-founder of the institute and Pakistan's renowned top nuclear scientist, recognizing my dedicated services at IBS.

February 2019

A Fond Farewell

My tenure at IBS concluded on good terms, closing a remarkable chapter of five years filled with learning, growth, and meaningful connections with countless patients and colleagues.
